## 1. Brand Overview

### Brand Name
**VitaRevive** — "Vita" (life) + "Revive" (to restore, renew). The name signals renewal and vitality without clinical undertones.

### Tagline Options

1. **"Your body already knows how to heal. We just remind it."**
2. **"Feel like you again."**
3. **"Science-backed. Body-first. Unapologetically you."**
4. **"Wellness that works with your biology."**
5. **"Reclaim your energy. Restore your glow."**
6. **"The upgrade your body's been waiting for."**
7. **"From surviving to thriving."**

**Recommended primary tagline:** #2 — "Feel like you again." It's emotional, simple, and speaks directly to the core audience who feels they've lost something.

### Brand Mission Statement

> VitaRevive exists to give women access to cutting-edge wellness tools — rooted in science, wrapped in warmth — so they can feel strong, energized, and fully themselves at every stage of life.

### Brand Voice & Tone Guide

| Attribute | We ARE | We are NOT |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| **Warm** | Your knowledgeable best friend | A lab coat lecturing you |
| **Confident** | Clear, assured, evidence-informed | Hype-y, MLM energy, miracle claims |
| **Approachable** | Conversational, real, relatable | Dumbed-down or condescending |
| **Empowering** | "You deserve to feel amazing" | "You're broken and need fixing" |
| **Premium** | Elevated but not exclusive | Luxury-gated or snobby |

**Voice rules:**
- First person plural ("we") when talking about the brand. Second person ("you") when talking to the customer.
- Use em-dashes liberally. They feel conversational.
- Short paragraphs. Breathing room. Let copy breathe.
- Never use "anti-aging" — use "pro-vitality" or "renewal."
- Avoid jargon unless immediately explained in plain language.
- Contractions always (we're, you'll, it's). Never stiff.

**Tone shifts by context:**
- **Instagram:** Warm, playful, slightly aspirational. Like texting a friend who happens to know a lot about wellness.
- **Product pages:** Confident, clear, gently educational. "Here's what it does, here's why it matters to you."
- **Email:** Personal, encouraging, like a note from someone who genuinely cares.
- **Compliance/legal areas:** Still warm but precise. Professional without being cold.

#### Secondary Colors
| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|------|-------|-----|-------|
| **Soft Gold** | Warm, muted gold | `#C9A96E` | Accents, highlights, premium touches. Foil on packaging. |

#### Accent Colors
| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|------|-------|-----|-------|

| **Eucalyptus** | Light sage-mint | `#D4DDD2` | Dividers, subtle backgrounds, hover states. |

#### Color Don'ts
- ❌ No electric/neon greens — we're not a juice bar
- ❌ No royal blue or navy — too corporate/clinical
- ❌ No pure black (`#000000`) — use Deep Forest (`#2D3B2D`) instead
- ❌ No pure white (`#FFFFFF`) — use Warm Cream or Linen White
- ❌ No red — too aggressive, too pharma

### Typography

#### Heading Font: **Playfair Display**
- Google Fonts: `Playfair Display`
- Weights: 400 (Regular), 500 (Medium), 700 (Bold)
- Usage: H1–H3, hero text, feature callouts, pull quotes
- Why: Elegant serif with personality. Feels premium and feminine without being fussy. The italic is beautiful for accent phrases.

#### Body Font: **DM Sans**
- Google Fonts: `DM Sans`
- Weights: 300 (Light), 400 (Regular), 500 (Medium), 700 (Bold)
- Usage: Body copy, navigation, buttons, UI elements, product descriptions
- Why: Clean, geometric, highly readable. Modern without being cold. Pairs beautifully with Playfair.

#### Accent/Script Font (sparingly): **Cormorant Garamond Italic**
- Google Fonts: `Cormorant Garamond` (Italic)
- Weight: 300 (Light Italic) only
- Usage: Taglines, testimonial callouts, single-word accents. MAX 5 words at a time.
- Why: Adds organic elegance. Use as spice, not the main course.

#### Typography Scale (Web)
```
H1: Playfair Display 700, 48px/56px
H2: Playfair Display 500, 36px/44px
H3: Playfair Display 400, 28px/36px
H4: DM Sans 700, 20px/28px
Body: DM Sans 400, 16px/24px
Small: DM Sans 400, 14px/20px
Button: DM Sans 500, 14px/14px, letter-spacing: 0.5px, uppercase
```

### Logo Direction

#### Concept 1: "The Living Wordmark"
A refined wordmark where **"Vita"** is set in a lighter weight and **"Revive"** is in a heavier weight or slightly different treatment — creating a visual "before and after" within the name itself. A single, delicate leaf or sprout emerges from the "i" in Vita or curves subtly off the "V" in Revive. The leaf should feel like it *grew* from the letterform, not like a sticker placed on top. Typography-forward. Think Aesop meets goop.

**Mark/Icon:** The standalone leaf-from-letter element. When extracted, it reads as an elegant, organic mark — a single leaf with a subtle stem that echoes the curves of the "V."

#### Concept 2: "The Renewal Circle"
The full word "VitaRevive" in a clean, elegant typeface. The icon/mark is a circular emblem featuring an abstract leaf that also suggests a double-helix or molecular bond — nature meeting science. The circle is NOT a full closed circle; it has a subtle opening or break (suggesting openness, possibility, not a closed system). Gold and sage green in a two-tone treatment. Feels like a wax seal or embossed emblem. Premium, almost apothecary.

**Mark/Icon:** The circle emblem alone. Works beautifully as a favicon, app icon, or embossed on packaging.

#### Concept 3: "The Elegant Monogram"
A stylized "VR" monogram where the V and R share a stroke — they're intertwined. Organic, flowing lines. Think less corporate monogram, more botanical illustration meets modern design. The full "VitaRevive" appears alongside in a clean serif. The monogram can incorporate a subtle botanical element — a leaf vein pattern within one of the letter strokes, or a tiny sprout at the intersection.

**Mark/Icon:** The VR monogram. Compact, distinctive, works at any size.

#### How "Vita" and "Revive" Should Relate Visually
- They are ONE word, not hyphenated, not separated by a space
- If differentiated typographically, it should be subtle — a weight change, not a color change
- "Vita" can be slightly lighter/thinner, "Revive" slightly bolder — mirroring the journey from depleted to restored
- NEVER split onto two lines in the primary logo
- The "V" is the most important letter — it anchors the brand. Give it room.

#### Logo Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**
- ✅ Keep generous clear space around the logo (minimum: height of the "V" on all sides)
- ✅ Use Deep Forest (`#2D3B2D`) on light backgrounds
- ✅ Use Linen White (`#FEFCF9`) on dark backgrounds
- ✅ Allow the gold (`#C9A96E`) as a single-color accent version for foil/emboss
- ✅ Design for embossing — the logo should work debossed into packaging
- ✅ Ensure the icon/mark works at 16x16px (favicon)

**Don't:**
- ❌ No gradients in the logo
- ❌ No drop shadows or bevels
- ❌ No rotating or skewing
- ❌ Don't place on busy photographic backgrounds without a container/backdrop
- ❌ Never use more than 2 colors in the logo simultaneously
- ❌ No outlined/stroked-only versions — the logo should always have fill
- ❌ Don't add a TM symbol in consumer-facing creative (keep it on legal/packaging fine print only)

#### Required Logo Variations
1. **Primary horizontal** — Full "VitaRevive" wordmark + icon mark to the left
2. **Stacked** — Icon on top, "VitaRevive" below. For square formats.
3. **Icon only** — The mark/emblem alone. For favicons, app icons, social avatars, packaging emboss.
4. **Wordmark only** — Just "VitaRevive" text, no icon. For tight horizontal spaces.
5. **Full + tagline** — Primary horizontal with tagline beneath. For packaging, letterhead.

Each variation in: Deep Forest on Cream, Cream on Deep Forest, Gold (single color), and Sage Green (single color).

### Photography & Imagery Style Guide

#### Mood
Warm, golden-hour light. Natural. Lived-in luxury — not sterile, not staged. Think: a beautiful kitchen in the morning, sunlight coming through linen curtains. A woman mid-laugh, not mid-pose. Effortless wellness, not performative wellness.

#### Lighting
- Soft, warm, natural light. Golden hour preferred.
- Light and airy — not moody or dark.
- Soft shadows. No harsh contrasts.
- Indoor shots: near windows, natural light flooding in.

#### Subjects
- **Primary:** Women ages 30–60. Diverse in body type, ethnicity, and age. REAL-looking. Not overly retouched.
- Beautiful but relatable. Think: the most put-together mom at school pickup, not a Victoria's Secret model.
- Showing moments of *ease* — stretching in the morning, laughing with friends, pouring tea, walking outside.
- Hands holding products, close-ups of textures, botanical elements.
- Lifestyle over product: the product enhances the scene, it doesn't dominate it.

#### What to Avoid
- ❌ Gym/fitness imagery (too aggressive, signals weight loss shame)
- ❌ Before/after photos (compliance risk + feels diet-culture-y)
- ❌ Lab coats, syringes, vials with colored liquids (too clinical)
- ❌ Stock photo smiles — forced, dead-eyed "wellness" faces
- ❌ All-white/sterile backgrounds (we're warm, not clinical)
- ❌ Overly young models (under 25) — our audience won't see themselves
- ❌ "Perfect body" imagery — no flat stomachs as the hero

#### Product Photography
- Products shot on natural surfaces: marble, linen, light wood, dried botanicals alongside
- Soft cream/sage backgrounds
- Subtle props: eucalyptus stems, gold accents, ceramic dishes, soft fabrics
- Hero product shots with soft natural shadows — not floating-on-white Amazon style
- Packaging should feel like it belongs in a beautiful bathroom or kitchen shelf

### Key Messaging Pillars

#### Pillar 1: "Your Biology, Your Ally"
Your body isn't failing you — it just needs the right support. Peptides are messengers your body already recognizes. We're not adding something foreign; we're amplifying what's already there.

#### Pillar 2: "Science You Can Feel"
We don't ask you to take our word for it. Every product is backed by published research, and we'll explain it in plain English. Transparency isn't a marketing buzzword for us — it's the whole point.

#### Pillar 3: "Designed for Your Life"
This isn't a complicated protocol. It's not a lifestyle overhaul. It fits into *your* life — the busy one, the real one. Wellness should feel like a gift to yourself, not another chore.

#### Pillar 4: "You Deserve to Feel Like You Again"
Somewhere between the demands of life and the passage of time, you lost a version of yourself that had more energy, more ease, more *aliveness.* She's not gone. She's just waiting for the right support.

### Elevator Pitch (30 seconds)

> "VitaRevive makes premium peptide wellness products designed specifically for women. Peptides are naturally occurring molecules your body already uses — we just give it more of the ones that support energy, recovery, and vitality. Think of us as the bridge between cutting-edge science and the warm, approachable wellness experience you actually want. No lab coats. No bro-science. Just real results, beautifully delivered."

### How to Talk About Peptides Without Being Clinical/Scary

**The framework: "Already in you → backed by science → here's what you'll feel"**

❌ **Don't say:** "BPC-157 is a synthetic peptide derived from gastric juice that promotes angiogenesis and tendon healing through modulation of the nitric oxide system."

✅ **Do say:** "Your body already makes BPC-157 — it's a natural healing peptide found in your gut. Research shows it supports tissue recovery and gut health. Most women notice less joint stiffness and better digestion within the first few weeks."

**Key language shifts:**
| Instead of... | Say... |
|---------------|--------|
| Peptide therapy | Peptide wellness |
| Injection / subcutaneous | Easy self-application |
| Dosage / protocol | Your daily ritual |
| Clinical studies show | Research suggests / Science supports |
| Treatment | Support / Solution |
| Anti-aging | Pro-vitality / Renewal |
| Synthetic | Bio-identical / Body-recognized |
| Side effects | What to expect |
| Patient | Member / You |

## 5. Social Media & Content

### Instagram Aesthetic Direction

**Grid feel:** Warm, cohesive, airy. Alternating between lifestyle photography, educational carousels (on-brand backgrounds), product flat-lays, and quote/text graphics. The grid should feel like flipping through a beautiful wellness magazine.

**Filter/editing style:**
- Warm tones, slightly lifted shadows
- Soft grain is okay (adds warmth)
- Never oversaturated. Never cool-toned.
- Consistent use of brand colors in graphics (Sage, Cream, Gold, Blush)
- Text-based posts: Cream background with Deep Forest text, or Sage background with Cream text

**Grid ratio:** For every 9 posts:
- 3 lifestyle/photography
- 3 educational carousels or infographics
- 2 product or unboxing content
- 1 testimonial or quote

### Content Pillar Topics

1. **"Peptides 101"** — Demystifying peptides. What they are, how they work, why they matter. Always approachable, never preachy. Carousel-heavy.

2. **"Feel Like You Again"** — Emotional storytelling. Customer journeys (anonymized or with permission). The *feeling* of getting your energy back. Reels and story content.

3. **"The Science Shelf"** — Deeper dives into research. Citing actual studies but translating them. Builds trust with the Sarah persona who needs proof. Carousels and blog links.

4. **"Rituals & Routines"** — How products fit into real life. Morning routines, self-care moments, the 2-minute daily ritual. Product integration without being salesy. Reels and lifestyle photos.

6. **"Community & Culture"** — User-generated content, team behind the brand, values-driven posts. Builds belonging. Photos, Reels, Lives.

## 7. Compliance Notes

### Staying Compliant While Maintaining Wellness Branding

**The core tension:** Many peptides are sold as "for research use only" or require prescriptions. VitaRevive must navigate this carefully depending on the regulatory pathway chosen (compounding pharmacy partnership, telehealth model, etc.).

**Key principles:**

1. **Never claim to treat, cure, or prevent any disease.** Full stop. No exceptions. No "wink-wink" language.
2. **Structure as a telehealth wellness platform** where possible — customers consult with a licensed provider, receive a prescription/recommendation, and products are fulfilled through a licensed compounding pharmacy.
3. **Educational content is your best friend.** You can educate about peptides and their studied benefits. You cannot say "our product does X to your body."
4. **Separate education from sales.** The science page educates. The product page invites. They can link to each other, but the product page doesn't make clinical claims.
5. **Testimonials must be genuine and include disclaimers.** "Individual results may vary" is the bare minimum.

### Disclaimer Language That Doesn't Kill the Vibe

**Product page (bottom, styled tastefully in small text matching brand fonts):**
> *These statements have not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease. Individual results may vary. Consult your healthcare provider before beginning any new wellness regimen.*

**Blog/educational content (end of article):**
> *The information provided is for educational purposes only and is not intended as medical advice. Always consult with a qualified healthcare provider regarding any health concerns or before starting any new supplement or wellness protocol.*

**Social media (in bio + as needed on posts):**
> 🔬 Science-backed wellness | Not medical advice | Always consult your provider
> *Results shown are individual experiences and may vary.*

**Styling tips for disclaimers:**
- Use the same brand fonts, just smaller and lighter weight
- Sage green or muted text — not jarring red or black bold
- Integrate naturally into the design — don't make it look like a legal afterthought
- On Instagram, use the last slide of a carousel rather than cramming it into a caption

### What to NEVER Say

**Absolute red lines — these will get the brand in legal trouble:**

- ❌ "Cures" / "Treats" / "Prevents" any condition
- ❌ "FDA approved" (unless it actually is — semaglutide is for specific uses, but YOUR product formulation may not be)
- ❌ "Guaranteed results" / "Works for everyone"
- ❌ "Lose X pounds in X weeks" — specific outcome claims
- ❌ "Reverses aging" / "Anti-aging treatment"
- ❌ "No side effects" — everything has potential side effects
- ❌ "Doctor recommended" without actual, documented, named physician endorsement
- ❌ "Better than [specific drug/competitor]" — comparative efficacy claims
- ❌ "Clinically proven" unless you have YOUR OWN clinical trials (citing others' research ≠ clinically proven for your product)
- ❌ "All natural" for synthetic/bio-identical peptides
- ❌ Anything implying customers don't need their doctor
- ❌ "Safe" as an absolute — say "well-studied" or "well-tolerated in research"
- ❌ Any claim about children or pregnancy

**Language to be cautious with (use only with proper context):**
- ⚠️ "Weight loss" — say "metabolic wellness" or "healthy weight management support"
- ⚠️ "Heals" — say "supports recovery" or "promotes your body's natural repair processes"
- ⚠️ "Boosts immunity" — say "supports immune resilience"
- ⚠️ "Hormone optimization" — say "supports hormonal wellness"
